DeVI: Physics-based Dexterous Human-Object Interaction via Synthetic Video Imitation

Hyeonwoo Kim1, Jeonghwan Kim1, Kyungwon Cho1, Hanbyul Joo1
Seoul National University1
Preprint
Paper Code arXiv

Given a physics environment with 3D human and objects along with an interaction text prompt, VMP generates a physically plausible human-object interaction motion by using a video diffusion model as an interaction-aware motion planner.


Abstract

Recent advances in video generative models enable the synthesis of realistic human-object interaction videos across a wide range of scenarios and object categories. While the human-object interaction knowledge embedded in these synthetic videos shows potential for motion planning in robotic manipulation, their limited physical fidelity and purely 2D nature make them difficult to use directly as imitation targets. We present VMP (Video as a Motion Planner), a novel framework leveraging text-conditioned synthetic videos to enable physically plausible agent control for interacting with unseen target objects. To address the imprecision of generative 2D cues, we introduce a hybrid tracking reward that integrates 3D human tracking with robust 2D object tracking. Unlike methods that depend on high-quality 3D kinematic demonstrations, our VMP relies solely on the generated video, enabling zero-shot generalizations across diverse objects and interaction types. Extensive experiments demonstrate that VMP outperforms existing approaches that imitate 3D human-object interaction demonstrations, particularly in modeling dexterous hand-object interactions. We further demonstrate the effectiveness of VMP in multi-object scenes and text-driven action diversity, highlighting the advantage of using video as an HOI-aware motion planner.


Method Overview



Our method consists of three parts; (1) 2D HOI Video Generation, (2) Extracting Hybrid Imitation Targets from the Video, and (3) Learning Humanoid Control Policy. First, we generate 2D HOI Video from the rendered 3D scene using the pre-trained image-to-video diffusion model. pipeline. Then, the Hybrid Imitation Targets which includes 3D human reference and 2D object reference is extracted from the video. Using the hybrid imitation targets, we learn humanoid control policy imitating the video via our hybrid tracking reward.
